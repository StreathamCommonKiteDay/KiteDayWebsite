The Highlight of South London's Calendar

The sky's the limit
on Streatham Common

Every May, Streatham Common transforms into a festival of colour, community and creativity. Watch world-class kite professionals perform breathtaking displays while you fly your own kite, browse local food stalls, and soak up the buzz of one of South London's most beloved free events.

Officially opened by the Mayor of Lambeth, the day brings together expert kite flyers, fantastic food, local community groups, kids' activities and — weather depending — some truly spectacular skies. Whether you're a seasoned kite enthusiast or bringing little ones for their very first taste of kite flying, there's something magical for everyone.

On the Day

Running Order

Here's how the day unfolds — though as any kite flyer knows, it all depends on the wind! ☁️

11:00am

🎉 Official Opening by the Mayor of Lambeth

The gates open, stalls come to life, and background music sets the mood. All approved stalls are open — let the festivities begin!

11:30am

🪁 Kite Displays Begin

Expert kite flyers take to the arena. Professional teams perform their routines to music — expect incredible skill, massive kites, and plenty of "wow" moments. Displays run for around 1.5 hours and repeat throughout the day.

All Day

🌤️ Large Static Kites in the Sky

Enormous static kites including NASA-designed lifters, wildlife shapes and unusual characters will be in the sky all day long. Look up!

All Day

🍕 Food, Drink & Shopping

Grab a wood-fired pizza, a curry, a Pimm's, a hog roast, an ice cream and more. Browse the kite stalls and community stands whenever the mood takes you.

All Day

🎪 Kids' Play Zone

Bouncy castles, assault courses, bungee trampolines and face painting — the play zone is open all day to keep little ones thoroughly entertained.

2:00pm

🏆 Kite Competition Judging

Think your homemade kite has what it takes? From 2-3pm our photographer will be on special lookout for the best looking and best flying homemade kites - creativity counts! Winners and prizes to be announced around 4pm. One hour of high-flying rivalry!

5:00pm

👋 Close of Day

The kites come down, but the memories last forever. See you next year! Don't forget to share your photos: #StreathamKiteDay

World-Class Talent

Meet the Pro Kiters

From kite ballet to NASA-grade lifters, this year's line-up will leave you speechless. Displays run throughout the day in the arena — watch out for their time slots!

🐟
Flying Fish
Dual Line Pairs · Southampton

Lex and Irma fly their kites in perfect synchrony to music — think kite ballet. Named after their first pair, Neptune and Jaws, they've been flying together since 2018 and were crowned UK National Champions in Dual Line Pairs. Utterly mesmerising.

🌈
Team Spectrum
Precision Flying · Triple Kite Master

Carl is one of the very few people in the world who can fly three two-line kites simultaneously to music. His display of control has astonished Streatham crowds for many years. Prepare to have your mind blown.

🚀
Nasaman — Keith
Power Kiting · NASA Lifters

Keith's extraordinary kites were originally designed by NASA to assist with the descent of space capsules. Many square metres of pure power — sometimes he has to be tethered down. Watch as he attempts to harness the wind and lift off (but not quite into orbit!).

🦅
Brighton Kite Flyers
Giant Display Kites

Bringing a spectacular collection of massive kites of all shapes — wildlife, fantasy creatures and unusual characters. These enormous inflatables fill the sky and delight crowds of all ages. You can't miss them!

🧵
Indian Paper Kite Flyers
Traditional Kite Flying

Look way, way up — and you might spot delicate Indian paper kites dancing on the thinnest button thread. A skilled flyer can make them dash and dart even when they're barely visible. Keep an eye out across the common.

🪁
Fly Your Own!
That Means You

Kite Day isn't just for the experts. Bring your kite, or pick one up from our pop-up kite shops. Flying a kite is one of the most joyful, relaxing pastimes you can share with your family. Come and have a go!

Fun For All

Kids Go Wild

Kite Day is a full-on family adventure. We've got activities to wear out even the most energetic little humans, plus a homemade kite competition for creative types of all ages!

🪁

Learn to Fly a Kite

There's no better place to learn than Streatham Common! Grab a kite from our pop-up shops and get those strings in the air. Experts are on hand to help.

🏆

Kite Competition

Put your imagination to work! Prizes for the best-looking and best-performing homemade kites. Judging starts at 2pm in the arena — all ages welcome.

🏰

Bouncy Castles & Assault Courses

Our Play Zone has bouncy castles and assault courses to challenge even the bounciest of children. Hours of fun guaranteed.

🤸

Bungee Trampolines

Want to feel like you're flying? Our bungee trampolines are the closest thing to kite flying with your feet off the ground!

🎨

Face Painting

Run by the brilliant Streatham Common Paddling Pool Volunteer Group. All money raised goes towards keeping the paddling pool open for the community.

Everything You Need

Plan Your Visit

We want your day to be as easy and enjoyable as possible. Here's everything you need to know before you arrive.

🚂

Getting Here

  • Streatham Station (Southern) — 5 min walk
  • Streatham Common Station (Southern) — 5 min walk
  • Multiple bus routes: 109, 159, 249, 250, 255
  • We encourage public transport to reduce local congestion
♿

Accessibility

The Common is largely flat and accessible for wheelchairs and buggies. Some areas of grass may be uneven — wear appropriate footwear. Accessible toilets will be available on site.

🚻

Facilities

  • Toilets available on site
  • Wide range of food and drink stalls
  • First aid team on site throughout the day
  • Dog-friendly event — keep them on leads near the kite arena!
💡

Top Tips

  • Bring your own kite (or buy one on the day!)
  • Wear layers — it can be breezy on the Common
  • Bring cash as well as cards
  • Aim to arrive early — kite displays run from 11:30am
  • Tag your photos: #StreathamKiteDay
